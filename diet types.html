Healthy Diets

Dash Diet

The dash diet is actually a type of healthy diet without the use of very special drinks and foods. What is important in the dash diet is the ingredients we use need to be low in salt, low in saturated fat and rich in potassium, calcium, magnesium, fiber and protein. It needs to be rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grain. It helps to lower blood pressure. You should limit your alcohol intake, sweet and sugar-sweetened beverages and foods high in saturated fats. Full-fat dairy, fatty meals, tropical oils, and most packaged snacks can be an example of saturated fats. You can determine how many calories you should consume each day with the aid of your healthcare expert. Your calorie requirements depend on your age, sex, amount of exercise, health issues, and whether you're attempting to lose weight or keep it off. There are multiple benefits of dash diet. Following this diet plan may help you to lower your blood pressure, reduce the possibility of developing heart disease, heart failure, and stroke. It may help prevent or control type 2 diabetes. It improves your cholesterol levels and reduce the chance of kidney stones.

Mediterranean Diet

A diet known as the Mediterranean Diet places a strong emphasis on plant-based meals and healthy fats. If you follow this diet type you need to consume lots of whole grains, vegetables, fruits, lentils, beans and nuts. There should be a lot of extra virgin olive oil (EVOO) as a healthy fat source. A reasonable amount of fish, especially omega-3-rich fish is important. Also other important points are; choosing poultry over red meat, eating little to no meat. Little to no butter, sugary beverages, or dessert and modest wine consumption with meals. Depending on your medical history, underlying diseases, allergies, and preferences, a nutritionist can assist you in modifying this diet as necessary. According to researches, eating a Mediterranean-style diet can reduce your chance of developing heart disease and many other chronic illnesses.Since there is no standard definition, you can modify the Mediterranean Diet to suit your needs.

Unhealthy Diets

Water Diet/Water Fasting

The fact that the body burns extra calories to balance the temperature of the water when drinking cold water has made the water diet more preferable for those who want to lose weight. It is a type of diet that is frequently preferred by those who want to lose weight fast, and it gives effective results when applied correctly, and it can also cause harm when used incorrectly. The water diet is a type of diet that creates a detox effect for the body by consuming plenty of water and liquid beverages and provides weight loss by increasing calorie burning. Excess fluid consumption may increase kidney stone formation as it will adversely affect kidney functions. The water diet has many harms, especially in long-term use. It can cause low blood pressure and nausea as it can cause blood sugar levels to drop. Because the body's nutritional needs are not met adequately, it can cause fainting, hair loss, loss of coordination, strength and endurance. May cause irregular eating habits in those with eating disorders.

No Sugar Diet

A sugar-free diet, commonly referred to as a no-sugar diet, limits additional sugar. However, this is not typically advised as part of a healthy, balanced diet. More extreme variants of a no sugar diet may include exclude items with naturally occurring sugar, such as fruits and vegetables. Despite official guidelines to cut back on the quantity of sugar you add to food and your consumption of sweetened beverages and foods, it is unrealistic and unhealthy to completely cut out sugar and carbohydrates from your diet. You run the danger of anemia and constipation, as well as feeling tired and short on energy, if you avoid starchy carbohydrates. Your bones may be at danger of osteoporosis if you stop eating dairy products without replacing them with calcium rich alternatives. We also know that eating five servings of a variety of fruits and vegetables each day is beneficial for heart health and cancer prevention, so avoiding fruit is not a good choice.
